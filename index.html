Our Mission

Battle Born Outdoors (BBO) is a 501(c)(3) non-profit organization dedicated to providing therapeutic outdoor experiences for disabled veterans 100% free of charge. We believe that the restorative power of nature, combined with the camaraderie of shared adventure, plays a vital role in healing and promoting long-term well-being.

Our mission is to empower disabled veterans through transformative outdoor experiences, fostering resilience, community, and reintegration into civilian life. We achieve this through carefully planned and executed programs that cater to a diverse range of physical and emotional needs. These adventures include, but are not limited to, snowmobiling, fishing excursions, hunting trips, and hiking events. Each event is tailored to ensure safety, accessibility, and a powerfully positive experience for all participants.

Beyond the physical activities, Battle Born Outdoors fosters a strong sense of community. Our programs are carefully designed to encourage connection, shared experiences, and mutual support among veterans. These moments of camaraderie contribute to significant therapeutic benefits, fostering confidence, resilience, and lasting friendships.

We are committed to providing a supportive and inclusive environment where veterans can heal, connect, and rediscover their strength. We understand the unique challenges faced by disabled veterans, working passionately to make our programs accessible and adaptable to a wide range of needs. Through the powerful combination of nature and community, we help our veterans find their way back to health and happiness.


Our Team

Derick Stephens

Founder and Executive Director of Battle Born Outdoor, Derick is dedicated to supporting veterans through transformative outdoor experiences. With 14 years of military service in the United States Marine Corps, Derick profoundly understands veterans' challenges after returning to civilian life. His firsthand experience has fueled his commitment to creating an organization that offers veterans healing and connection through activities like hunting, fishing, hiking, and camping.

Philip Krabbe

Co-founder Philip Krabbe is a proud United States Marine Corps Veteran who has demonstrated resilience and courage both in and out of uniform. After serving his country, Philip became an advocate for fellow veterans, dedicating his time to sharing his story and offering support to those who have faced similar challenges. As a National Volunteer Speaker for the Wounded Warrior Project, he works to inspire and empower other veterans, using his experiences to shed light on the importance of recovery, strength, and community. Philip's commitment to service continues through his efforts to bring hope and healing to those who have sacrificed for our country.

“Healers are Spiritual Warriors who have found the courage to defeat the darkness of their souls. Awakening and rising from the depths of their deepest fears, like a Phoenix rising from the ashes. Reborn with a wisdom and strength that creates a light that shines bright enough to help, encourage, and inspire others out of their own darkness.”
- Melanie Koulouris
